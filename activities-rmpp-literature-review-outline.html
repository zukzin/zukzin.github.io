Unit 4: Literature Review and Research Proposal Outlines

Literature Review and Research Proposal Outline

This literature review examines cybersecurity threats in IoT-enabled hospitality systems and evaluates existing risk management frameworks (Chaudhry, Ahmed & Alghamdi, 2023). The aims are to:

  • Identify key cybersecurity risks in smart hotels and IoT-based operations.
  • Assess the effectiveness of frameworks like NIST, GDPR, PCI DSS, and AI-driven security solutions.
  • Analyse research gaps in IoT security within hospitality due to rapid technological advancements.
  • Propose future research directions to enhance IoT security in hotels.

The review is intended for:

  • Academics and researchers in smart hospitality cybersecurity.
  • Hospitality professionals, IT managers, and policymakers.
  • Technology developers and IoT security consultants.

The increasing adoption of IoT in hotels makes the industry a cyberattack target. Cybercriminals exploit vulnerabilities in:

  • Smart room systems (keyless entry, voice assistants).
  • Hotel management platforms (online booking, payment processing).
    • Public Wi-Fi networks exposing guest data.

High-profile breaches like the Marriott data breach (2018) and DarkHotel attack highlight the need for stronger cybersecurity frameworks. This review:

  • Identifies emerging threats in IoT-based hospitality systems.
  • Assesses regulatory and compliance challenges.
  • Contributes to developing comprehensive IoT security strategies.

Context, perspective, and theoretical framework

Hotels leverage IoT to enhance guest experiences but face increasing cybersecurity threats, leading to financial and reputational damage. The review follows a technological risk assessment approach, focusing on:

  • IoT vulnerabilities in hospitality.
  • Cybersecurity policies and frameworks for hotels.
  • Future technological developments in IoT security.

The theoretical framework follows a multi-layered cybersecurity approach integrating:

  • NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)
  • ISO 27001 Information Security Standard
  • NCSC, OWASP, GDPR, PCI DSS

Layers:

  • Perception Layer - Device-level threats (e.g., smart locks, RFID keycards).
  • PNetwork Layer - Data transmission risks (e.g., Wi-Fi exploitation, DDoS).
  • PProcessing Layer - Cloud security issues (e.g., ransomware, unauthorized access).
  • PApplication Layer - Guest-facing platform vulnerabilities.
  • PService Management Layer - Privacy and compliance challenges.

Selected Sources and Inclusion Criteria. Databases: IEEE Xplore, ScienceDirect, Springer, ACM Digital Library, ProQuest, NDLTD.

Inclusion Criteria: Studies (2021-2024) on IoT security in hospitality. Empirical research on cyberattacks in hotels.

Exclusion Criteria: Studies before 2018 (unless foundational). General IoT security research unrelated to hospitality.

Review Structure:

  • Introduction: Overview of IoT in hospitality and cybersecurity significance.
  • Existing Cybersecurity Frameworks: NIST, PCI DSS, GDPR, AI, Blockchain solutions
  • IoT Cybersecurity Risks: Layered approach detailing threats.
  • Gaps and Limitations: Lack of IoT-specific models, AI-based monitoring, and Zero Trust challenges.
  • Conclusion and Recommendations: Strategies for IoT security, future research on AI and Blockchain authentication

Main Findings

  • Most hotels lack standardized cybersecurity frameworks.
  • IoT adoption increases cyber risks.
  • Wi-Fi attacks like DarkHotel target business travelers.
  • AI-driven threat detection enhances intrusion detection.
  • Blockchain authentication strengthens guest data security.

Strengths:

    Comprehensive discussions on IoT threats in hotels. Research on AI and Blockchain solutions. Real-world cyberattack studies (e.g., Marriott breach).

Limitations:

  • Few hospitality-specific IoT security studies.
  • Limited empirical evidence on AI-based security.
  • Gaps in third-party vendor risk assessment.

Discrepancies in Literature

    Debate over AI-driven security effectiveness (some argue AI helps, others highlight zero-day attack risks). Differing views on GDPRâ€™s success (some see improved protection, others note compliance struggles).

Conclusions

  • Hospitality faces major cybersecurity challenges due to IoT adoption.
  • Current cybersecurity frameworks are insufficient for hotels.
  • AI, Blockchain, and Zero Trust offer promising security solutions.

Future Research Directions

  • Developing hospitality-specific IoT cybersecurity models.
  • Enhancing AI-driven threat detection.
  • Investigating third-party vendor security risks in hotel IoT ecosystems.
