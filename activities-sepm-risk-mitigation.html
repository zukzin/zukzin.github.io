Unit 4: Risks and risk mitigation

Read the articles by Verner et al (2014) and Anton & Nucu (2020) and then answer the following questions:

  1. What are the main risks that the authors identify? How do these fit into the traditional SDLC model?
  2. Which of the frameworks discussed in the Unit 3 lecturecast would you use to capture and categorise the risks?
  3. Add a risk and a suggested mitigation to the module wiki.
  4. Choose an entry made by one of your colleagues in the wiki and comment on how you might mitigate it.

RESPONSE: MAIN RISKS THAT THE AUTHORS IDENTIFY

Verner et al. (2014) identifies risks in Global Software Development (GSD), focusing on communication issues, time zone differences, and cultural misunderstandings. These risks are categorised into four areas:

  • project management
  • software development
  • human resources
  • outsourcing rationale

    • The biggest risks stem from project management challenges, including coordination difficulties due to geographic and temporal separation of teams. Communication breakdowns are exacerbated by cultural and language barriers, leading to delayed project timelines and misaligned expectations.

    Anton & Nucu (2020) focus on Enterprise Risk Management (ERM) and identify risks related to strategic misalignment, financial distress, and operational inefficiencies. They highlight risks associated with regulatory compliance and suggest that ERM's effectiveness depends on proactive identification and management of these risks.

    RESPONSE: FITTING RISKS INTO THE TRADITIONAL SDLC MODEL

    In the traditional Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC), the identified risks can be mapped across different phases:

  • Planning & Requirements Gathering: Risks in early planning stages - unclear project scope (GSD) and strategic misalignment (ERM).
  • Design & Development: Poor communication and coordination, hindering design and development efforts, causing delays and errors.
  • Testing: Misunderstandings due to cultural and language differences could lead to incomplete or incorrect test cases in GSD.
  • Deployment & Maintenance: Operational risks, in ERM context, affect the long-term sustainability and maintenance of the system.

    • RESPONSE: FRAMEWORKS FOR RISK CAPTURE AND CATEGORIZATION

    Frameworks like COCOMO and Critical Chain Project Management (CCPM) can be useful in capturing and categorizing risks in both software development and enterprise-wide contexts. Additionally, the Risk Management Process (RMP) from the lecture offers a structured approach to handling risks through identification, analysis, evaluation, and treatment.The PDCA (Plan-Do-Check-Act) cycle, mentioned in the lecture, fits well into both GSD and ERM contexts for continuous risk monitoring and mitigation.

    RESPONSE: RISK FOR THE WIKI AND SUGGESTED MITIGATION

  • Risk: Time zone differences in GSD leading to communication delays.
  • Mitigation: Implementing asynchronous communication tools (Slack, Trello) to allow teams to stay updated.
  • Establishing a rotating schedule for synchronous meetings can also help align team members across different time zones
