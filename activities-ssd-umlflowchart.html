Formative Activities: Unit 2

This is unit 2 formative activity. The topic here is based on the continuation of the collaborative discussion on UML Flowchart.

Unit 2: UML Flowchar

Continue the discussion in unit 1. You should choose at least two of your peers' contributions and comment on the weakness they chose, as well as:

  1. What do you like about their flowchart?
  2. In what way(s) might it be improved?

The first chosen chart is by Anda Ziemele

What do I like about their flowchart

Her flowchart provides a clear and logical breakdown of cryptographic failures and aligns well with the SDLC phases. The inclusion of specific steps, such as ensuring secure internet protocols (e.g., HTTPS) and encrypting stored data with secure algorithms, demonstrates a thorough understanding of key cryptographic concepts. Highlighting potential consequences like GDPR non-compliance and specific algorithm weaknesses (e.g., MD5) adds depth to her analysis and enhances the flowchart's educational value.

In what way(s) might it be improved?

To further strengthen her flowchart, I think she should consider:

  1. Visual clarity: Add distinct labels or colour codes for each SDLC phase (e.g., Planning, Design) to visually emphasise the transition between stages.
  2. Actionable items: Include specific examples or recommendations in decision points, such as "Evaluate encryption algorithms against OWASP standards" or "Conduct regular audits for compliance with GDPR and PCI-DSS."
  3. Error handling: Incorporate a step or decision point for addressing potential failures, such as a feedback loop to reevaluate cryptographic protocols in case vulnerabilities are identified post-deployment.

The second chosen chart is by Oi Lam Siu

What do I like about their flowchart

Her activity diagram effectively highlights key vulnerabilities in the website login process, and the choice of an activity diagram is well-justified. The focus on visualizing the workflow and decision points makes it easy to follow and understand for both technical and non-technical audiences. By addressing specific weaknesses like "Permitting Unlimited Failed Login Attempts" and "Lack of Multi-Factor Authentication," her work demonstrates a strong grasp of the potential threats associated with identification and authentication failures.

In what way(s) might it be improved?

To further enhance her activity diagram I believe she should:

  1. Incorporate mitigating actions: Add specific steps or actions to address vulnerabilities at each stage. For example, after identifying "Permitting Unlimited Failed Login Attempts," the diagram could include a branch for "Lock account after X failed attempts."
  2. Error-handling paths: Introduce explicit error paths or conditions, such as what happens when MFA is unavailable or when an account enumeration attempt is detected.
  3. Visual clarity: Use distinct symbols or annotations to differentiate between vulnerabilities and mitigation points. This would make it easier for viewers to distinguish between problems and proposed solutions.
