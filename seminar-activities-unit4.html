Seminar Activities: SEPM

Unit 4 Seminar: Estimating Tools and Risk Assessment

Acitvity 1:

Review the NIST Privacy tools. How do these fit with the risk assessment methods and tools described in last week’s Lecturecast?. Create a Python program that implements one of the estimation methods covered in the Lecturecast. You can use the Jupyter Notebook workspace (see software installation below) and save your work to your GitHub repository.

Based on the requirements you have gathered for your assignment, create an estimate of the total effort and time to complete the planned demonstration of your system. For the seminar session, be prepared to share your estimates and the reasons why you selected your chosen estimation method. Use the python program you have created to calculate your estimates.

Software Installations

We will be using Jupyter Notebook to write and run Python scripts. Download the latest version of Anaconda. You can also use the Jupyter Notebook in the cloud. However you need to sign in with an account credential to access this tool. Alternatively you can access the site via Jupyter.org.

Activity 2:

Also, read the articles by Verner et al (2014) and Anton & Nucu (2020) and then answer the following questions:

  1. What are the main risks that the authors identify?
  2. Which of the frameworks discussed in the Unit 3 Lecturecast would you use to capture and categorise the risks?
  3. Add a risk and a suggested mitigation to the module forum.

RESPONSE TO THE REVIEW ON NIST PRIVACY TOOLS

The NIST Privacy Framework emphasizes a privacy-centric approach to risk management by focusing on data actions and privacy impacts (NIST, 2020). This aligns with the general risk identification methods discussed in the Unit 3 lecture, which also stress a systematic approach to gathering and categorising risks (Unit 3 Lecture Cast).

Similarities:

  1. Risk Identification: In the Unit 3 lecture cast, the emphasis is on identifying risks from various perspectives, it mentions risks like notification mailboxes and threat workshops. Similarly, NIST’s Privacy Framework stresses identifying privacy risks by focusing on data actions that could lead to privacy problems for individuals (NIST, 2020). Both methods are systematic approaches to gathering and categorising risks across an organisation's different facets.
  2. Risk Analysis: In the Unit 3 lecture cast, risk analysis involves assigning risks to either a qualitative or quantitative assessment. NIST's framework encourages using a contextual understanding of risk likelihood and impact, focusing on problematic data actions and their consequences.
  3. For example, NIST discusses the challenges in assessing privacy impacts on individuals and the subjective nature of privacy risks, which mirrors the subjectivity discussed in Unit 3's qualitative risk assessments.
  4. Risk Mitigation: Both frameworks share common methods of mitigating risk. Unit 3 lecture cast mentions options such as eliminating, reducing, tolerating, or transferring risks. NIST’s framework echoes these, offering strategies to mitigate, transfer, avoid, or accept privacy risks based on a detailed understanding of the impact on both individuals and organizations.

    5. Differences:

    1. Privacy-specific Risks: NIST’s approach is more privacy-centric, focusing on data processing risks that may lead to tangible and intangible harms to individuals (e.g., embarrassment, discrimination). Unit 3's lecture cast’s framework focuses more broadly on business risks, including technical vulnerabilities, business impact assessments, and security audits.
    2. Regulatory Focus: NIST emphasizes compliance with privacy regulations (e.g., GDPR) and ethical considerations. This adds a layer of complexity when compared to Unit 3's lecture cast which has a broader risk assessment, and does not focus as explicitly on regulatory compliance or ethical considerations.
    3. Structured Privacy Risk Models: NIST introduces specific tools like the Privacy Risk Assessment Methodology (PRAM) to assess privacy risks, which are not part of the general risk assessment approaches in Unit 3’s lecture cast. This makes NIST’s approach more suited for privacy professionals.

    In conclusion, the NIST Privacy Framework provides more specialized guidance on privacy risks, focusing on privacy-centric data processing issues, while Unit 3’s lecture cast general risk management principles that can be applied more broadly across various organizational risks. Both emphasize structured, repeatable methods for identifying, analyzing, and mitigating risks

  6. National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) (2023) SP 800-226: Guidelines for Evaluating Differential Privacy Guarantees. Available at: https://csrc.nist.gov/publications/detail/sp/800/226/draft (Accessed: 20 September 2024).
  7. Near, J., Darais, D., Lefkovitz, N., Howarth, G. (2023) NIST.IR.8062: An Introduction to Privacy Engineering and Risk Management in Federal Systems. National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST). Available at: https://www.nist.gov/privacy-framework (Accessed: 20 September 2024).
