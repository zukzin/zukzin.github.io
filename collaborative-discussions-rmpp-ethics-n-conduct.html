Research Methods and Professional Practice

Contribution by Zukiswa Tuso

The Medical Implant Risk Analysis case study from the Association of Computing Machinery (ACM) (ACM, 2018) is very close to me and it deals with life-critical technology wirelessly connected medical implants like pacemakers and also insulin pumps.

The dilemma is clear, if a computing professional discovers a serious security vulnerability in the devices, meaning hackers could potentially tamper with them, putting patients or our loved ones' lives at risk.

The ethical question to ask is - "What should they do?"

Should they immediately report the issue, risking corporate backlash, or stay silent and hope the company addresses it later?

This situation directly relates to the ACM Code of Ethics, particularly principles like public interest, avoiding harm, honesty, professional responsibility, and privacy.

Ignoring the problem could be dangerous, while speaking up might cause legal and professional challenges for the organisation in question. The legal implications are also significant for example in the U.S., the FDA regulates medical device security, while in the UK/EU, the MHRA oversees it. So if security flaws lead to harm, lawsuits or penalties could follow. The BCS Code of Conduct aligns with ACMâ€™s ethics, reinforcing that public safety must always come first.
