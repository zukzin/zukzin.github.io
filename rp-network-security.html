This unit is based on my reflections network security and how I navigated through the different programmes to be able to understand the importance of regular monitoring in network security.

Embarking on the Journey of Lifelong Learning

A Reflective Account of Personal and Professional Growth in understanding the complexities surrounding cybersecurity tools, rules and concepts.

Jared Cohen

We live in a world where all wars will begin as cyber wars... It's the combination of hacking and massive, well-coordinated disinformation campaigns..

In the aftermath of the Black Screen of Death (BSOD) incident caused by Microsoft on July 19th, which disrupted global IT operations, grounded flights, and stalled healthcare and banking services, this module has become exceptionally relevant to me. This experience has intensified my interest in cybersecurity, although I am still uncertain about whether I want to pursue it as a career. Nonetheless, I thoroughly enjoyed the readings and even began watching the series "Mr. Robot" on Netflix, along with other cybersecurity-related content. My awareness and interest in this field have significantly increased.

When I commenced work on Module 4, I had serious reservations about my ability to complete it, especially after reading Unit 6. Initially, I felt the requirements exceeded my capabilities. However, I decided to persevere and methodically go through the reading list. As with all modules, particularly when the subject matter is new, understanding the terminology, researching definitions, and making sense of the content took considerable time and effort. Finding sources that provided clear explanations and better understanding was a time-consuming process.

I vividly recall my attempts to download Linux. When I read Leroux's article on the Kali Linux Review, the statement, "If you read the article but skipped the parts containing too much technical jargon, Kali is not for you," made me feel momentarily defeated. I paused for a day or two to reflect on what I had read. Determined to overcome this challenge, I decided to download Kali Linux. After several attempts and some trial and error, I successfully downloaded and installed VirtualBox. This experience boosted my confidence, affirming that I could tackle this task. Finally, when I successfully uploaded VirtualBox and used it with Kali Linux, I was able to perform the scan on my chosen site, https://34.246.169.176/.

This accomplishment was a significant milestone in my learning journey, reinforcing my capability to navigate and understand complex cybersecurity tools and concepts.

    One of the critical lessons I have also learned from this module is the importance of preventing reoccurrences of issues related to encryption keys and encrypted databases. Encryption is a fundamental aspect of protecting sensitive data, but it is not foolproof if not managed correctly.

    Frequent Password Changes

    Another key insight is the necessity of enforcing frequent password changes within to organisations stakeholders. Passwords are the first line of defence against unauthorised access, and static passwords become vulnerable over time. Implementing a policy that requires employees to update their passwords regularly can significantly reduce the risk of password-related breaches (NIST, 2020). Furthermore, educating employees about creating strong, complex passwords and the dangers of password reuse is crucial in strengthening overall security posture.

    Enhanced Monitoring Tools and Intrusion Prevention Systems (IPS)

    My analysis also highlighted the need for better monitoring tools and the integration of advanced Intrusion Prevention Systems (IPS). Effective monitoring tools can provide real-time alerts and detailed logs, allowing for the quick detection and response to potential security incidents. Implementing an IPS can further enhance security by proactively identifying and blocking suspicious activities before they can cause harm (Andress and Winterfeld, 2013). These systems are vital for maintaining the integrity of the network and ensuring continuous protection against evolving threats.

    Defence Tools like Microsoft Defender for Backend SQL Databases

    Utilising defence tools such as Microsoft Defender for backend SQL databases is another critical strategy that emerged from my assessment. These tools offer advanced threat protection by continuously monitoring and analysing database activities for signs of malicious behaviour. By leveraging such technologies, organizations can ensure that their databases are safeguarded against unauthorised access and data breaches, providing an additional layer of security (Leroux, 2020).

    Assessing Organizational and Website Weaknesses

    A comprehensive understanding of an organization's or website's weaknesses is understanding the fundamentals of network security, the importance of regularly checking for open ports and missing configurations, which can sometimes be easy entry points for attackers.

    Firewall Implementation

    Ensuring a robust security system is in place is another critical aspect of network security. Firewalls serve as a barrier between internal networks and external threats, filtering out malicious traffic and preventing unauthorised access. My reflection on this part analysis stresses the importance of configuring and maintaining firewalls properly for effective protection (NIST, 2020).
